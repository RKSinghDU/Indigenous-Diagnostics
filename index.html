SLIDE 7

Indigenous Diagnostic Framework

Four Lenses for Organizational Diagnosis

1. SVADHARMA

(Unique Purpose)

Diagnostic Question:

Is organization fulfilling its unique purpose?

2. PRAKRITI

(Organizational Nature)

Diagnostic Question:

What is the innate character/DNA of this organization?

3. KALA

(Timing/Readiness)

Diagnostic Question:

Is this the right moment for change? Organizational readiness?

4. INTERCONNECTION

(Atman-Brahman)

Diagnostic Question:

How do all parts affect the whole system?

SLIDE 8

Two Ways of Seeing Organizational Problems

Diagnostic Question Western Frameworks (SWOT, Force Field) Indigenous Approach (Vedantic Lens)
What's the problem? External threats, internal weaknesses Misalignment with organizational svadharma
Who's affected? Stakeholders (separate entities) Interconnected web (all affect all)
When to intervene? ASAP (urgency) Kala (right timing, readiness)
How to diagnose? Analyze parts (functional silos) See whole system (prakriti)

SLIDE 11

Structural Interventions in OD

DEFINITION:

Interventions that change the FORMAL organizational design—hierarchy, roles, departments, processes, work design

FOUR MAIN TYPES:

1. RESTRUCTURING
  • Changing hierarchy (flatten/add layers)
  • Departmentation (functional → matrix)
  • Reporting relationships
2. DOWNSIZING / RIGHTSIZING
  • Reducing organizational size
  • Streamlining operations
3. REENGINEERING
  • Process redesign (eliminate non-value steps)
  • Technology integration
4. WORK DESIGN ◄── Today's Focus
  • Job redesign (top-down)
  • Job crafting (bottom-up)
SLIDE 12

Decentralization Principle (Gandhian OD)

"True democracy or the Swaraj of the masses can never come through untruthful and violent means... The structure of world federation can be raised only on a foundation of non-violence, and violence will have to be totally abjured from world affairs."

— Gandhi (Gram Swarajya)

OD APPLICATION:

✓ EMPOWER

  • Decentralize
  • Flatten hierarchy
  • Increase autonomy

✗ CENTRALIZE

  • Increase control
  • Reduce employee agency

CRITICAL QUESTION:

Does this structural change align with SWARAJ (self-governance) and SARVODAYA (welfare of all)?

PART 3

JOB REDESIGN & JOB CRAFTING

The Most Employee-Impacting Structural Intervention

SLIDE 15

Top-Down vs. Bottom-Up Work Design

JOB REDESIGN (Top-Down) JOB CRAFTING (Bottom-Up)
Approach Management analyzes jobs and redesigns them Employees reshape jobs proactively
Framework Job Characteristics Model (Hackman & Oldham, 1976) Based on individual agency and meaning-making (Wrzesniewski & Dutton, 2001)
Characteristics • Fast implementation
• Standardized changes
• Management controls		 • Slower, organic change
• Personalized adaptations
• Employee autonomy
Example Tata Motors Sanand - Assembly line workers also do quality checks (enrichment) Manufacturing worker mentors new hires (relational crafting)
SLIDE 16

Job Redesign Framework: Hackman & Oldham (1976)

5 CORE JOB DIMENSIONS

  1. Skill Variety
  2. Task Identity
  3. Task Significance
  4. Autonomy
  5. Feedback
→

CRITICAL PSYCHOLOGICAL STATES

  • Meaningfulness
  • Responsibility
  • Knowledge of Results
→

OUTCOMES

  • High Motivation
  • High Performance
  • High Satisfaction
  • Low Absenteeism
  • Low Turnover

MODERATOR: Growth Need Strength (individual difference)

Presenter Notes

"This model guides HOW to redesign jobs for enrichment"

SLIDE 17

Job Crafting: Employees as Active Shapers

Wrzesniewski & Dutton (2001)

1. TASK CRAFTING

Changing job boundaries, tasks added/removed

Example: Nurse adds patient education to routine care duties

2. RELATIONAL CRAFTING

Changing who you interact with and how

Example: Manufacturing worker becomes informal mentor to new employees

3. COGNITIVE CRAFTING

Changing how you perceive/frame your work

Example: Janitor sees work as "creating healing environments" not "cleaning"

CONNECTION TO KARMA YOGA:

Finding NISHKAMA KARMA (intrinsic meaning) in work

SLIDE 18

Which Approach Aligns with Gandhian Principles?

SARVODAYA (Welfare of All)

Job Redesign: Benefits all in role (standardized)

Job Crafting: Benefits individual (personalized)

→ Which ensures universal benefit?

SWARAJ (Self-Governance)

Job Redesign: Management decides

Job Crafting: Employee decides

→ Which honors self-determination?

TRUSTEESHIP

Job Redesign: Management as stewards improving work

Job Crafting: Employees as stewards of their own meaning

→ Different manifestations of stewardship?

TENSION:

Job redesign can be PATERNALISTIC
Job crafting embodies SWARAJ but may lack structure

SYNTHESIS: Use BOTH strategically!

Critical Slide

SLIDE 23

In Bharat Textiles Simulation Round 2...

When selecting STRUCTURAL INTERVENTIONS, ask:

  • INDIGENOUS DIAGNOSTIC
    Does this honor organizational SVADHARMA?
    What's the PRAKRITI (nature) of this department?
  • JOB CHARACTERISTICS
    Does it increase skill variety, autonomy, feedback?
    Will it create meaningfulness and ownership?
  • EMPLOYEE AGENCY
    Can we enable JOB CRAFTING alongside redesign?
    Are we empowering or controlling?
  • GANDHIAN ALIGNMENT
    Does it serve SARVODAYA (welfare of all)?
    Does it honor SWARAJ (self-governance)?
    Are we stewards (TRUSTEESHIP) of employees?
  • SYSTEMS THINKING
    What are ripple effects across departments?
    How does this affect organizational wholeness?

BACKUP SLIDE 3

Does Job Crafting Actually Work?

META-ANALYTIC FINDINGS:

✓ Positive effects on:

  • Job satisfaction (r = .38)
  • Work engagement (r = .46)
  • Performance (r = .23)
  • Organizational commitment (r = .35)

MODERATORS:

  • Growth need strength (individual difference)
  • Organizational support for autonomy
  • Job complexity (more complex = more crafting opportunity)

CHALLENGES:

  • Can create role ambiguity
  • May disadvantage low-agency employees
  • Requires managerial support

SYNTHESIS:

Job crafting works best as COMPLEMENT to good job design, not REPLACEMENT for structural improvement

Source: Rudolph et al. (2017) meta-analysis, Journal of Vocational Behavior
